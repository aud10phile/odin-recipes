Rustic White Loaf

Description

This recipe is for a basic white loaf of bread in a rustic style. I started the loaf in a stand mixer but it is also easy to start by hand, on the counter, the old fashioned way. I took this recipe from Paul Hollywood's book 100 Great Breads but like most recipes that I bake, I modify them. I would say the main modification for this recipe is the "rustic" look of the loaf. I've made this recipe in a tin previously and it makes delicious sandwich bread.

Some other things to note. I like my bread salty. A nice salty finish to a bread is something that really excites me (maybe I have a problem). If you don't like a salty bread, I would use Unsalted butter. As you'll see in the instructions, I don't activate my yeast before putting it into the dough. This means that having room temperature everything will not slow down your initial rise. With that, here is what I used as ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 500g White Bread Flour
  • 270ml Water (Room Temperature)
  • 57g Salted Butter (Softened)
  • 10g Salt
  • 10g Active Dry Yeast

Instructions

  1. Start by combining all of dry ingredients in the mixing bowl.
  2. Turn your stand mixer on low (or start digging in with your hands) and slowly add your softened butter.
  3. Now slowly add your water and mix until all ingredients are no longer sticking to the side of the mixing bowl.
  4. At this point, you have two options. Start your proof or be like me and turn out your dough onto your table and knead until you're satisfied with the gluten structure.
  5. Let your dough rise for an hour or until doubled in size.
  6. Prep a Banneton(or proofing basket) with a bit of flour.
